Mission & Vision Statements

Your mission statement is the “why” behind your company—its primary purpose for existing—while a vision statement is focused on your goals for the future. When writing these statements, make them brief so visitors can easily understand your priorities. Include information on why your brand exists and what makes you different from other companies in your industry. This can be accomplished by highlighting the unique challenges your company aims to solve or the unique approach you take to solving them.

Company Values

Including your values on your About Us page is key for building trust with potential customers. Company values are the set of guiding principles behind your business, and they can help customers determine whether your brand aligns with their own needs and values. Your list of company values should state the core ideals your business stands for in clear and simple terms.

Story

Telling the story of your business helps humanize your brand and shows how you got where you are today. When outlining your companys history, make sure to briefly overview how your business came to be and your goals for the future. Dont be afraid to also highlight specific challenges your company faced and how you overcame them. For variety, consider displaying this information visually, such as with a timeline showing your business origin and growth through the present day.

Service

Listing the specific services you provide shows people what it is you do and helps customers identify why your company is the best fit for them. Because your companys website will also have a separate, more detailed service page, keep the services overview in the About Us section brief. Additionally, use your services to highlight what makes you stand out from other companies in your industry, such as specific needs youre meeting in your local community.

Final

Wrap up the About Us section of your website with a call-to-action, directing customers to the next steps in the buyers journey. Some tips for writing an effective CTA include using action words (like “schedule an appointment now”), sparking curiosity, and implementing first-person language.
